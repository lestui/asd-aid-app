# ASD Helper Guide — Logo & SVG Image Design Brief

## Project Overview

**App name:** ASD Helper Guide  
**Product type:** Web app / helper guide  
**Main audience:** Adults supporting autistic children, including parents, caregivers, teachers, aides, and support workers.  
**Purpose:** The app helps adults support autistic children with communication, routines, sensory needs, emotional regulation, meltdown support, shutdown support, and everyday care strategies.

The visual identity should feel calm, practical, warm, trustworthy, and easy to understand. It should be suitable for a real support tool, not a toy, medical clinic, or overly childish product.

---

## Overall Brand Direction

The ASD Helper Guide brand should communicate:

- Calm support
- Clear communication
- Practical help
- Emotional warmth
- Safety and trust
- Guidance for adults helping children

The design should be simple enough to work in a web app interface, as SVG illustrations, card images, buttons, headers, and app icons.

---

## Core Visual Style

Use a **clean modern SVG-friendly flat vector style**.

Visual characteristics:

- Rounded shapes
- Soft corners
- Clean spacing
- Minimal detail
- Gentle human figures
- Simple symbols
- Friendly but professional appearance
- Strong readability at small sizes
- No cluttered backgrounds
- No heavy shadows
- No complex gradients
- No realistic photo style

The artwork should be consistent across the logo, icon, and helper-card illustrations.

---

## Colour Direction

Use a calm web-app-friendly palette:

### Main Colour
Soft calming blue.

Suggested uses:

- Logo main shape
- Speech bubble
- Parent/child figures
- Card illustration outlines or major forms
- Buttons or UI accents

### Accent Colour
Warm orange.

Suggested uses:

- Heart symbol
- Highlighted action steps
- Small visual emphasis
- Important but gentle attention points

### Background Colour
Use off-white, very light blue, or very light warm neutral backgrounds.

### Colour Requirements

- Keep contrast accessible and readable.
- Orange should be an accent only, not the dominant colour.
- Avoid bright chaotic colour combinations.
- Avoid harsh red warning-style visuals unless absolutely necessary.

# Global Design Rules

Apply these rules to every logo, icon, and illustration:

- Use soft blue as the main brand colour.
- Use warm orange only as an accent.
- Keep all images simple, rounded, and SVG-friendly.
- Use minimal facial detail.
- Show support, space, communication, and calm choices.
- Avoid puzzle pieces.
- Avoid dramatic distress, fear, chaos, or shame-based imagery.
- Avoid medical/clinical harshness.
- Avoid cluttered backgrounds.
- Avoid overly childish cartoon style.
- Avoid bright chaotic colours.
- Make images suitable for parents, teachers, carers, and support workers.
- Keep the full set visually consistent as part of one web app design system.

---

# Recommended Final Logo Direction

Use this final logo structure:

**Header logo:** Icon on left + “ASD Helper Guide” on right.

**Icon:** Rounded speech bubble containing a simple parent/child shape and a small orange heart.

This works because it clearly communicates communication, support, care, and guidance without relying on puzzle-piece imagery. It feels appropriate for a practical web app used by adults supporting autistic children.
